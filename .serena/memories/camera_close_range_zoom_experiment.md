# Close-range camera zoom — tried & reverted (negative result)

Date: 2026-06-27. Source research doc: `docs/camera-scanning-improvements.md`.
Design spec (with Outcome section): `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-06-27-close-range-camera-tuning-design.md`.

## What we tried
Implement the WWDC21 `AVCamBarcode` technique in `CameraService`: compute a
`videoZoomFactor` from `minimumFocusDistance` so the sharp focus distance lands outside
the wide-cam dead zone (more pixels on the collector strip → better OCR). Made it
runtime-toggleable (`closeRangeZoom` @AppStorage in DeckListView Debug section) to A/B
against driver auto-macro on-device. AF hints (`continuousAutoFocus` +
`autoFocusRangeRestriction = .near`) were always-on; the toggle gated only the zoom.

## What the device data showed (iPhone 14 Pro `soPro`, via devicectl --console)
At `videoZoomFactor=1.0`:
```
minFocus=20  fov=106.2  range=[1.0, 123.75]  applied=1.0  (already focusable)
```
- `minimumFocusDistance` = **20 mm** (millimeters), NOT the ~200 mm the research doc
  assumed for the wide cam.
- `fov=106°` = the **ultra-wide** constituent. At 1.0× the `builtInTripleCamera` serves
  ultra-wide, which already focuses to ~2 cm. **No focus dead zone exists on this device.**
- The WWDC21 formula correctly returns *no zoom* (desired framing distance ≈79 mm is
  outside the 20 mm dead zone → "already focusable"). ON and OFF produced **identical
  1.0× captures**; scans fast & indistinguishable in both arms.

## Conclusion (durable)
The deterministic-zoom technique solves a large-dead-zone problem the 14 Pro does NOT have
— auto-macro via the ultra-wide already gives sharp ~2 cm focus. The zoom is **inert** here.

## What shipped vs reverted
- **Reverted:** `CloseRangeZoom` seam, the `videoZoomFactor` logic, the `closeRangeZoom`
  toggle.
- **Kept:** `CameraService.configureForCloseRange(_:)` reduced to the AF hints only
  (`continuousAutoFocus` + `.near`), called from `startSession()` after `startRunning()`.
  Strict, free improvement so AF stops hunting toward infinity on close cards.

## If collector-number OCR ever proves weak
The remaining lever is **pixel density**, not focus: force a zoom onto the wide/tele
constituent for more pixels on the strip (at 1.0× you're on the wide-FOV ultra-wide, so the
strip is small in frame). Deferred until a real OCR-failure case justifies it. Also see the
research doc's other low-cost wins (higher-res activeFormat, rectOfInterest).
